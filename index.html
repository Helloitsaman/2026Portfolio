01 — About

My
Story

Mumbai, India · Since 2006

I started my journey building small bots before I fully understood what I was doing, eventually building bigger and better bots.

What began as curiosity evolved into a precise technical discipline embedded systems, autonomous control, PCB layout, and sensor fusion. Every project added a new layer to how I think about hardware and code together.

Already two years into engineering, this isn't just a hobby. It is the lens through which I see every problem: how do systems communicate, respond, and behave in the physical world?

06 — Timeline

How I got
here.

2024

Started Engineering College and Participated in the Robosumo Competition of FRCRCE - RoboRift 2024

Built DEADBOLT 69, my first Bluetooth-controlled bot. Spent nights learning motor control, serial communication, and the patience that hardware demands winning the 1st round against 4th year students.

2024 — 2025

Joined Project Cell Club and dove deeper into Embedded Systems

Built another Robosumo Bot, worked on a SpyderBot, built a peltier module AC, developed a cyber security encryption device on a Raspberry Pi Pico W. Transitioned from a beginner to a more proficient embedded systems developer.

2025

Started my own Robotics Club in FRCRCE - Robix

Established Robix, the official robotics and automation club at FRCRCE, built an incredible community of likeminded individuals, hosted robotics events and participated in multiple competitions.

2025 — 2026

Grew Robix further and continued exploring different fields

Became an SIH Hardware Edition Finalist, BitNBuild International Hackathon Finalist, MumbaiHacks Finalist, won Robosumo and Robosoccer competition in SPCE, started building the entire 32-bit RISC-V CPU from logic gates in Logisim Evolution while building personal projects to enhance my skills.
